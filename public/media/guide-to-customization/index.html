Hydro Flask - Guide to Flask Customization


When considering customizing your flasks with engraving or screen printing, please refer to the guide below where you'll find information about the Artwork Format, Process and Pricing. Contact your Account Manager with any additional questions.


1. ARTWORK FORMAT - VECTOR
 

Vector artwork is required for both engraving and screen printing. We will accept raster images but they will have to be vectorized (see illustrations below).
We do not accept .dst files!
When submitting vector artwork with text, make sure all of the fonts are outlined!

NOTE: If the artwork is not in vector format (meaning it's a raster image) a vectorizing fee of $45 may be applied.

2. ARTWORK PROCESS
  ENGRAVING
Artwork is converted into a one-color (black) element. The black portion of the artwork is "etched" into the flask's powder-coat and looks "silver".

NOTE: We do not engrave on Classic Stainless flasks (because there is no powder-coat).

 
  The original image
(submitted by customer)		 Image converted into a single-color element Detail view of the engraved artwork
IMPORTANT: Due to the limitations of the laser engraver machine there are some artwork restrictions:
- Lines must be at least 0.5 pt thick and fonts must be at least 12 pt or bigger.
- The width of the artwork is limited to 1.75in on 12, 18, 21 and 24oz flasks and 2.45in on 40 and 64oz flasks.
- For complex artwork, the final product may look slightly different than the virtual proof. Some detail may be lost.
  SCREEN PRINTING
Artwork can only be 4 colors or less (solid colors and no gradients). Pantone colors must be supplied or the closest match will be chosen.
